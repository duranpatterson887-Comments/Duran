CoinMarketCap's API runs on a **credit-based pricing model** — every successful API call consumes a set number of credits, and your monthly plan determines how many credits you have available. This design gives you full transparency into your API spend: you always know exactly how much each call costs, and you can predict your bill from your usage patterns. Whether you're prototyping on the free tier or running a professional trading platform at scale, there's a plan sized for you — and you can upgrade at any time as your data needs grow.

<Tip>
  Start with the free **Basic plan** to prototype without spending a cent. You get 15,000 credits per month — enough to make hundreds of price-quote calls daily — with no credit card required. Sign up at [pro.coinmarketcap.com/signup](https://pro.coinmarketcap.com/signup).
</Tip>

## How Credits Work

Every response from the CoinMarketCap API includes a `credit_count` field in the `status` object, showing exactly how many credits that call consumed.

```json
{
  "status": {
    "timestamp": "2025-01-15T12:00:00.000Z",
    "error_code": 0,
    "credit_count": 1,
    "elapsed": 14
  },
  "data": { "..." : "..." }
}
```

Credit costs vary by endpoint category and data richness:

| Endpoint Category | Typical Credit Cost |
|---|---|
| Cryptocurrency quotes (latest) | 1 credit per call |
| Cryptocurrency listings (latest) | 1 credit per call |
| Global metrics (latest) | 1 credit per call |
| OHLCV data (latest) | 1 credit per call |
| OHLCV data (historical) | 1 credit per interval returned |
| Cryptocurrency metadata | 1 credit per 100 tokens |
| Market pairs | 1 credit per 100 results |
| DEX pairs & pools | 1 credit per call |
| On-chain data | Varies by endpoint |

<Info>
  Credits are **not** deducted for failed requests that return authentication errors (error codes `1001`, `1002`, `1006`) or for requests that are rejected before processing. You only pay for successful data responses.
</Info>

### Credit Consumption Example

If you build a dashboard that polls BTC, ETH, and SOL prices every 60 seconds, you're making 3 calls per minute × 60 minutes × 24 hours = **4,320 credits per day** — well within the Basic plan's 15,000-credit monthly allowance.

For a production app polling 100 symbols every 30 seconds, you'd consume roughly 100 credits per minute × 43,200 minutes/month = **4,320,000 credits/month**, which maps to the Growth or Professional tier.

## Monitoring Your Usage

Keep an eye on your credit consumption directly in the CoinMarketCap developer dashboard:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the dashboard">
    Log in at [pro.coinmarketcap.com](https://pro.coinmarketcap.com) and navigate to **Dashboard → Usage**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your credit balance">
    The **Usage** panel shows your credits consumed this month, your remaining balance, and a day-by-day consumption chart so you can spot spikes early.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set usage alerts">
    Configure email alerts to notify you when you reach 80% or 100% of your monthly credit allocation — preventing unexpected interruptions to your application.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inspect per-endpoint breakdown">
    The detailed usage log shows which endpoints consumed the most credits, helping you optimise your integration — for example, by caching responses or batching symbol queries.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Batch your requests** to stretch your credit allowance further. The `/v2/cryptocurrency/quotes/latest` endpoint accepts a comma-separated list of symbols (`symbol=BTC,ETH,SOL,BNB`) and returns all of them for **1 credit** — far more efficient than individual calls per symbol.
</Tip>
